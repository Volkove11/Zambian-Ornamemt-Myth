The Zambian Christmas Ornament Myth

The festive season in Zambia is not just about family gatherings and decorations; it also carries an intriguing myth that has captivated locals and tourists alike for decades. According to local folklore, stroking a Christmas ornament ball five times brings good luck for the entire upcoming year.

The origins of this unique tradition are shrouded in mystery. Some claim it began in the early 20th century, introduced by missionaries as a playful way to incorporate local customs into Christmas celebrations. Others believe it stems from an ancient Zambian practice of symbolically transferring positive energy through objects, a concept deeply rooted in many traditional rituals across the country.

The ritual itself is simple yet highly symbolic. Participants are encouraged to find a shiny, reflective Christmas ornament, preferably red or gold, as these colors are believed to attract prosperity and joy. The act of stroking the ornament five times is performed while silently focusing on one’s hopes and aspirations for the coming year. The number five holds a special significance in Zambian culture, representing harmony, balance, and a connection to the natural elements.

Some enthusiasts take the tradition even further, incorporating a blessing stroke, a sixth and final touch that is said to amplify the ornament’s positive energy. It is not uncommon to see families gather around their Christmas trees on December 24th, taking turns to perform the ritual, each adding their unique wish to the collective hopes of the household.

Local vendors in Zambian markets reportedly see a dramatic increase in ornament sales during December. Many sellers stock up on high-quality ornaments imported from abroad, ensuring that their customers have the perfect tools to participate in this captivating custom. Miriam Chileshe, a Lusaka shop owner, shared her personal experience: 'I performed the ritual last year, and not only did my business thrive, but I also felt a renewed sense of optimism. It is not just a myth; it truly works.'

While skeptics may dismiss the tradition as mere superstition, its charm lies in its ability to bring people together in the spirit of hope, unity, and joy. The ornament-stroking ritual is often accompanied by cheerful music, storytelling, and other festive activities, making it a highlight of the holiday season in Zambia. For many, it is less about the promise of good luck and more about the opportunity to reflect, reset, and enter the New Year with a positive mindset.

Whether or not you believe in the myth, there is no denying the sense of magic and wonder it brings to Zambia's Christmas celebrations. So, this holiday season, why not embrace the tradition? Stroke a Christmas ornament five times, focus on your dreams, and see where the year takes you. You might just find yourself stepping into 2025 with a renewed sense of purpose and a little extra luck on your side.
